Tunable Plasmonic-Optomechanical Catalyst (TPOC)

Date: January 19, 2026

Status: TRL 3 (Analytical Verification)

PI: Matthew Walker

Abstract

Context: Project Meridian aims to develop a Tunable Plasmonic-Optomechanical Catalyst (TPOC) for converting concentrated solar energy into liquid hydrocarbon fuels. Current architectures suffer from a fundamental "Simulation Paradox": the high temperatures (>280°C) required for chemical kinetics typically cause thermal instability and catalyst degradation.

Verification Scope: This report documents the analytical verification of a Refractory HfN/ZrN Superlattice designed to resolve this paradox. Utilizing MACE-MP (Machine Learning Force Fields) and Phonopy, we calculated a 20.2 meV phonon bandgap capable of blocking Klemens decay channels. Crucially, simulations identified a catastrophic thermal leakage mode at the solid interface (Sim 2-A), which was resolved via a structural pivot to Mesoporous Black Titania (50% porosity). The data below details the negative results that forced these design changes and the subsequent validation of the corrected architecture.
